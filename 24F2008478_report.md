## 1. Introduction
This project focuses on the automated detection of 20 thoracic pathologies from Chest X-rays. The objective is to maximize the macro-averaged asymmetric cost score, where missing a disease (False Negative) is penalized with -5 points. The dataset contains 51,043 training images and 17,015 test images.

## 2. Methodology
- **Model:** DenseNet121 (Pre-trained). Chosen for its superior feature reuse in medical imaging.
- **Preprocessing:** 
    - **CLAHE:** Enhanced contrast for better pathology visibility.
    - **Augmentation:** Random Horizontal Flip to prevent overfitting.
    - **Normalization:** ImageNet standards.
- **Combined Loss:** I implemented a custom `CombinedAsymmetricLoss` using **Weighted BCE** (to handle the -5 penalty) and **Focal Loss** (to focus on hard-to-classify rare diseases).

## 3. Experiments and Results
- **Model Comparison:** Compared DenseNet121 and ResNet50; DenseNet showed better convergence for thoracic features.
- **Parameter Variations:** Tested LR 1e-4 and Batch Size 32. 
- **Validation Strategy:** Used 85/15 train-validation split to monitor overfitting behavior.
