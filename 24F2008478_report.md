## 1. Introduction
The objective of this project is to develop a deep learning model to classify chest X-ray images into 20 different thoracic pathologies. This is a multi-class problem where each image belongs to exactly one class. A unique challenge is the asymmetric misclassification cost, where a False Negative (missing a disease) results in a -5 penalty, while a False Positive results in -1. My goal was to maximize the macro-averaged asymmetric cost score.

## 2. Methodology
- **Model Choice:** I used **DenseNet121** as the backbone. I chose DenseNet because its feature-reuse capability is highly effective for identifying subtle medical patterns in X-ray images compared to traditional CNNs.
- **Preprocessing:** 
    - **CLAHE:** Applied for contrast enhancement to make lung pathologies more visible.
    - **Resizing:** Images were resized to 224x224.
    - **Normalization:** Applied standard ImageNet mean and standard deviation.
- **Data Augmentation:** Used Random Horizontal Flip and Rotation to prevent overfitting and improve generalization.
- **Transfer Learning:** Fine-tuned the pretrained DenseNet121 architecture for the 20 target classes.

## 3. Experiments and Results
I performed several experiments to optimize the model as per Step 4 and 5:
- **Model Comparison:** Compared **DenseNet121** and **ResNet50**. DenseNet consistently showed a lower validation loss.
- **Loss Function:** Implemented a **Combined Asymmetric Loss** (Weighted BCE + Focal Loss). The 5x weight for pathologies addressed the -5 penalty requirement, while Focal Loss focused on hard-to-classify rare diseases like 'Pneumomediastinum'.
- **Parameter Variations:** Tested Learning Rates (1e-3, 1e-4). 1e-4 provided stable convergence.
- **Incremental Training:** Initially trained on 224x224 resolution and planned for fine-tuning at 256x256.

| Experiment | Configuration | Validation Loss |
|------------|---------------|-----------------|
| Baseline | ResNet50 | 0.2215 |
| Fine-tuned | DenseNet121 + CLAHE | 0.1980 |
| **Final** | **DenseNet121 + Combined Loss** | **0.1827** |

## 4. Conclusion
The most critical factor was handling the asymmetric cost. By weighting pathologies 5x higher than 'No Finding', I minimized the -5 penalty impact. CLAHE preprocessing also played a vital role in model performance.
